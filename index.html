About BeerGenie

BeerGenie is a comprehensive drink counter and drinking habit tracker designed to help you monitor your alcohol consumption and build awareness around your drinking patterns. Whether you're at a bar, party, or home, BeerGenie helps you track every drink accurately and understand your consumption habits over time.

The app's primary function is drink counting and session tracking. You can log drinks, view your consumption history in a calendar format, track statistics, monitor dry streaks, set personalized daily limits based on CDC guidelines, and receive water reminders. All of these core features are available by default and work independently of any optional features.

BeerGenie also offers optional educational BAC estimates that you can enable in settings. These estimates are hidden by default and only appear if you explicitly choose to enable them after acknowledging appropriate disclaimers. These optional estimates are for educational and informational purposes only and should never be used to determine fitness to drive or operate machinery.

Standard Drink Reference

Standard drink definitions used in BeerGenie are based on guidelines from the National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA). One standard drink contains 0.6 fluid ounces (14 grams) of pure alcohol.

Sources:

  • National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA). "What Is A Standard Drink?" https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/alcohols-effects-health/overview-alcohol-consumption/what-standard-drink
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). "What is Excessive Drinking?" https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/fact-sheets/excessive-drinking.htm

Standard Drink Equivalents:

  • Beer: 12 oz @ 5% ABV = 1 standard drink
  • Wine: 5 oz @ 12% ABV = 1 standard drink
  • Cocktail: 4 oz @ 15% ABV = 1 standard drink
  • Shot: 1.5 oz @ 40% ABV = 1 standard drink
  • Hard Seltzer: 12 oz @ 5% ABV = 1 standard drink

Drinking Limits & Recommendations

Daily and weekly drinking limits recommended in BeerGenie are based on guidelines from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA). These recommendations are for moderate alcohol consumption and may not be appropriate for everyone.

Sources:

  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). "Dietary Guidelines for Americans: Alcohol." https://www.cdc.gov/alcohol/fact-sheets/moderate-drinking.htm
  • National Institute on Alcohol Abuse and Alcoholism (NIAAA). "Moderate & Binge Drinking." https://www.niaaa.nih.gov/alcohols-effects-health/overview-alcohol-consumption/moderate-binge-drinking
  • U.S. Department of Health and Human Services and U.S. Department of Agriculture. "2020-2025 Dietary Guidelines for Americans, 9th Edition." https://www.dietaryguidelines.gov/

Recommended Limits:

  • Men: Up to 2 drinks per day, 14 drinks per week
  • Women: Up to 1 drink per day, 7 drinks per week

Important Note: These recommendations are general guidelines and may not be appropriate for everyone. Individuals with certain medical conditions, those taking medications, pregnant women, and those under the legal drinking age should not consume alcohol. Always consult with a healthcare provider about alcohol consumption.
